What I Study

As a student pursuing both Data Science and Finance, I have had the opportunity to take a wide range of courses that have helped me build both technical and business skills. Some of the most relevant classes I have taken include Profit Analysis for Managers and Advisors, Large-Scale Storage and Retrieval, Corporate Finance, and Presentation and Data Visualization. Through these courses, I have developed a deeper understanding of how data and financial decision-making connect in real-world settings. One of the most valuable things I have learned from studying both subjects is how they complement each other. Data science has taught me how to work with large amounts of information, identify meaningful patterns, reduce bias, and communicate insights clearly through visualization. Finance, on the other hand, has shown me how those insights can be applied to evaluate companies, understand performance, and support strategic decisions. I have especially seen this connection in case competitions and academic projects, where I have been able to use my data science background to analyze financial statements and broader datasets more effectively. Being able to quickly recognize trends, organize information, and present conclusions clearly has become one of my biggest strengths. I believe this combination of technical and financial knowledge will be extremely valuable in the workplace and will help me stand out in future opportunities.
